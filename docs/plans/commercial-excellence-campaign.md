# From Claims to Keys: Branch Excellence Campaign

## Proposal for Hertz Insurance Replacement Operations

**Date:** February 2026
**Status:** DRAFT — for review and input

---

## 1. Context

Insurance Replacement is one of Hertz's highest-margin segments — 30%+ contribution margin, 18 average days on rent, and 100% warm leads. Our best branches convert at 75–80%+, and through recent data analysis we've identified the specific practices that drive those results.

---

## 2. The Opportunity

We have a wealth of best demonstrated practices (BDPs) across the network, but today, those learnings sit with individual branches. They haven't been systematically socialised, which means most of the organisation isn't yet benefiting from what our top performers have already figured out.

The opportunity is significant: closing the gap between our current network average (~67%) and what good looks like (75–80%+) represents substantial unrealised revenue. The key learnings span the full IR conversion journey:

- **Speed to contact** — top branches make first outbound contact within 30 minutes and rarely miss contacting their reservations
- **Lead ownership** — Checks HLES every 15 minutes and treat every lead as their own, relentlessly driving the process for conversion
- **Inbound responsiveness** — top branches always picks up the phone, and when busy, requests the customer to "please hold" rather than letting it ring out
- **Body shop relationships** — top branches maintain a positive working relationship with nearby bodyshops and proactively protects reservations from being flipped
- **Weekly leads review** — Branch managers reviewing pipeline and conversion regularly

This campaign is about **permeating those learnings across every branch** so that everyone benefits and everyone wins.

## 3. Proposed Initiative: "From Claims to Keys" Branch Excellence Campaign

### 3.1 Objective

Equip every branch manager and their direct reports (MAs and MTs) with the knowledge, tools, and accountability to execute Insurance Replacement operations at the standard of our best branches — targeting 75–80%+ conversion across the network.

### 3.2 Approach

A **16-week rolling programme** of structured sessions, delivered fortnightly on Wednesdays to **all Branch Managers and their direct reports (MAs and MTs)**. Session 1 is a kick-off meeting where Gim sets the vision and introduces the series. From Session 2 onwards, the programme transitions to **"Gim Unfiltered"** — a recorded, 30-minute podcast-style session on Microsoft Teams where Gim speaks to one topic per episode, often with a guest Branch Manager.

Each session focuses on one topic, uses real data and real examples, and ends with clear actions for participants to implement before the next session. This is not a training programme. It is a **campaign** — a sustained, visible, leadership-led push to raise the operational bar across the network. Think of it as a marketing campaign for operational excellence: continuous hype, continuous content, continuous momentum.

> **Note:** Area Managers and General Managers are not part of the session audience. Gim holds a **separate monthly cadence with AMs and GMs** to cover strategic oversight, cascade learnings, and address questions bubbled up from the branch-level sessions.

### 3.3 Design Principles

- **Led from the top.** Gim opens and anchors the programme. This signals organisational priority.
- **Peer-driven.** Top-performing branch managers join as guests and share what they actually do.
- **Personal.** Gim follows up directly — phone calls to branch managers, checking in on actions. The interaction happens 1:1, not in a broadcast setting.
- **One-way broadcast.** Sessions are delivered as a one-way broadcast: microphones and cameras OFF for attendees, Q&A turned off, chat turned off, reactions ON (hand clapping, laughing emojis). This keeps sessions tight and within 30 minutes. Common questions are bubbled up to the separate AM/GM monthly calls with Gim.
- **Action-oriented.** Every session ends with 1–2 specific things participants will do differently starting that week.
- **Recorded.** Every session is recorded on Teams and packaged as a podcast episode for on-demand listening, catch-up, and new starter onboarding.

## 5. Production & Distribution

This campaign is run like a **marketing campaign** — content doesn't just live in a single meeting. It has a life before, during, and after each session.

### Recording & Post-Production

- All sessions (kick-off + podcast episodes) are **recorded on Microsoft Teams**
- Recordings are packaged into **podcast-style audio episodes** with intro, outro, and a BDP "sponsor" segment (e.g., *"Today's episode is brought to you by Adam Frankel from Miami — here's what his branches do differently"*)
- Packaged episodes uploaded to **OneDrive** for on-demand access

### Hype & Anticipation

- Only **3 sessions foreshadowed at a time** (not the full series) — keeps anticipation fresh and allows for timing adjustments
- **Monthly touch base between Gim and Alicia** to review pacing, adjust upcoming session timing, and confirm guest availability
- One-pager and key artefacts **emailed personally from Gim** and posted at branches (HR boards / break rooms)

### Reminder Campaign (Per Session)

Each session follows a 3-touch notification cadence:

1. **Calendar invite** sent with session details and raffle mention
2. **Thumbnail update** — calendar invite updated with episode artwork (triggers a second notification)
3. **Pre-session reminder** — "tune in" message sent the day of or day before

### Post-Session Follow-Up

- **AI-generated casual summary** sent to all participants after each episode — written in a colloquial, friendly tone (not executive bullet points). Recaps the key takeaways in a way that feels like a mate telling you what you missed.
- **Viva Engage post** when the podcast episode goes live on OneDrive — opens a chat thread for feedback, reactions, and momentum between sessions

### Attendance Incentive

- Attendees (live or recording viewers) are **entered into a raffle to win Award Points** (gift card equivalent)
- Communicated in the calendar invite for each session

### Continuous Push

- The campaign does not rely on live attendance alone. Every session is repackaged and pushed out, ensuring the content reaches every branch manager regardless of whether they attended live
- New starters can catch up on the back catalogue of episodes
- This creates a **persistent, always-on presence** for the campaign — not a one-off meeting that fades from memory

## 6. AM/GM Monthly Cadence (Separate from Campaign)

General Managers and Area Managers are **not part of the session audience** for this campaign. Instead, Gim holds a **separate monthly call with AMs and GMs** to:

- Share campaign progress and key themes from recent episodes
- Discuss questions and concerns bubbled up from the branch-level sessions
- Align on strategic priorities and regional follow-up actions
- Review conversion performance at the GM/region level

This separation keeps the campaign sessions focused and tight (30 minutes, one-way broadcast) while giving GMs and AMs a dedicated forum for two-way discussion with Gim.
