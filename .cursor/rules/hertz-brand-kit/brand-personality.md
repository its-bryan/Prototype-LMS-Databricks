# Hertz Brand Personality & Design Principles

> Read this file before every design decision. This is the North Star.

## Brand DNA

Hertz is the world's first car rental company (est. 1918). Over a century of premium mobility. The brand communicates **confidence without arrogance**, **premium without pretension**, and **speed without recklessness**.

The Hertz Australia digital experience (hertz.com.au, managed in partnership with Mustard Creative) prioritizes conversion-driven clarity — helping users find, book, and get on the road as fast as possible.

## Voice

| Attribute | What it means | What it does NOT mean |
|---|---|---|
| **Confident** | Direct, assertive headlines. No hedging. | Aggressive, shouty, all-caps everything |
| **Premium** | Quality materials, refined spacing, polished details | Luxury-hotel ornate, gold-leaf decoration |
| **Efficient** | Every element earns its place. Ruthless editing. | Cold, robotic, stripped-bare |
| **Warm** | Approachable typography, human-scale imagery | Bubbly, cartoon-ish, emoji-heavy |
| **Australian-inflected** | Clean, outdoorsy, road-trip energy | Slang-heavy, overly casual |

## Visual Principles

### 1. Gold is the Weapon — Use it Surgically
The `#FFD100` gold is Hertz's signature. It should feel electric and purposeful:
- Use for **primary CTAs**, **active states**, **price highlights**, and **key navigation accents**
- NEVER flood a section with gold background — it becomes illegible and loses power
- Gold works best as a **stripe, underline, button fill, or badge** against black or white
- On gold backgrounds, text MUST be `#272425` black — never white

### 2. Black Carries the Structure
`#272425` (warm black) is the backbone of the interface:
- Navigation bars, headers, footer — black grounds the layout
- Large black type on white creates the bold, editorial feel Hertz needs
- Black + gold is the ONLY two-color combination that should feel "Hertz" at a glance

### 3. White is Breathing Room
- Card surfaces, content areas, and form backgrounds should be white or near-white
- Generous whitespace signals premium quality
- Don't fill every gap — let content breathe

### 4. Bold Typography Hierarchy
Hertz's brand typeface (a customized Interstate with a distinctive low H crossbar) is confident and geometric. In digital:
- **Headlines**: Large, bold (700–800 weight), tight letter-spacing. Make them hit hard.
- **Body text**: Clean, readable, medium weight. 16px minimum.
- **CTAs & Labels**: Semi-bold to bold, slightly wider letter-spacing in UPPERCASE for labels.
- The hierarchy should be obvious at a glance — if you squint at the page, you should still see the structure.

### 5. Cars Are the Hero
- Vehicle imagery should always be prominent, well-lit, and on clean backgrounds
- Never bury vehicles in cluttered layouts or behind heavy overlays
- When showing vehicle cards, let the car image be the dominant element — don't make text compete

### 6. Speed in Every Interaction
Inspired by Hertz's "one-click booking" philosophy (from their 2024 redesign concepts):
- Minimize form fields — pre-fill where possible
- Progress should feel forward-moving (stepper patterns, not modal traps)
- Transitions should be quick (150–250ms) and directional (slide in from the right for forward, left for back)

### 7. Precision Over Decoration
- Tight border-radii (4–8px), not rounded pills
- Minimal shadows — use for elevation hierarchy, not decoration
- No ornamental gradients, patterns, or textures unless they serve wayfinding
- Grid-aligned everything — automotive precision is the vibe

## Emotional Targets

When someone uses this app, they should feel:
1. **"I'm in good hands"** — trust through visual quality and clarity
2. **"This is fast"** — efficiency through streamlined flows
3. **"This is premium"** — elevated through bold typography and purposeful gold accents
4. **"I want to drive"** — excitement through hero vehicle imagery and road-trip energy

## Reference Mood

Think: **The confidence of a well-designed airport terminal** — clear wayfinding, premium materials, efficient flow, no clutter. Not a luxury hotel lobby (too ornate), not a tech startup dashboard (too playful), not a government website (too sterile).
